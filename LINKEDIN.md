3 days ago, this product did not exist.

Today, it’s live.

Over the last 72 hours, I spent 16+ hours a day building Taskman.

What started as a small CLI idea turned into a full product:

- a private daily workspace
- task management
- daysheet logging
- calendar integration
- Google auth
- live deployment
- full marketing video
- a real product identity

And the interesting part is this:

This didn’t come from startup brainstorming.
It came from real friction in my own workflow.

I was managing:
- tasks in TickTick
- calendar in a browser tab
- daysheets manually in Obsidian

It worked, technically.
But it was fragmented, noisy, and full of context switching.

So I built the thing I actually wanted to use.

Taskman is a private daily workspace that brings together tasks, daysheet logging, and calendar planning in one clean, focused system.

No bloated productivity noise.
No scattered workflow.
No juggling multiple tools just to hold one day together.

Just clarity.
Just momentum.
Just results.

These last 3 days reminded me of something simple:

Ideas matter less than execution.
Friction is often the best source of product direction.
And sometimes the fastest way to find the right tool is to build it yourself.

Taskman.

Have clarity. Build momentum. Get results.
